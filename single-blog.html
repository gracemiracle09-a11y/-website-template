XG's cocona came out as...

  • Travel, Lifestyle
  • 03 Comments

In January 2026, the music group XG officially announced that they were changing what their name stands for. Since their debut in 2022, XG had stood for “Xtraordinary Girls,” but the group revealed that they would now call themselves “Xtraordinary Genes.” The decision reflected a shift in how they wanted to represent themselves as they all entered their twenties and continued growing creatively and personally. The new meaning emphasizes the core creativity and individuality that fuels their music and identity, rather than focusing on the word “Girls.”

The name change also came shortly after one of the members, Cocona, publicly shared that they are transmasculine and nonbinary, which highlighted how the original name didn’t fully represent the group as it is now. By adopting “Genes” instead of “Girls,” XG aimed to make their name more inclusive while keeping the same acronym fans already know. This rebranding was more than just a new label — it marked a new era for the group as they prepared to release their first full-length album THE CORE and embark on a world tour early in 2026.
